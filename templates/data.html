Quick Safety Tips

Plan Your Route in Advance

• Before heading out, check maps and identify the safest and well-lit routes.
• Avoid shortcuts through isolated areas, especially late at night.
• Share your estimated time of arrival (ETA) with a trusted friend or family member.
• Keep your phone charged and ensure location services are enabled.
• If possible, plan backup routes in case of unexpected detours.
• Stay updated on traffic, weather, or local safety alerts.
• Let someone know once you safely reach your destination.

Trust Your Instincts

• Pay attention to your gut feelings—if something feels wrong, it usually is.
• Do not hesitate to change your path or step into a safer, crowded space.
• If a stranger makes you uncomfortable, create distance immediately.
• Trust your inner voice over social pressure or politeness.
• Call a friend, emergency contact, or helpline if you feel uneasy.
• Remember, prioritizing your safety is always more important than being polite.
• Never ignore small warning signs, as they can prevent bigger risks.

Carry Essentials

• Keep a fully charged power bank to ensure your phone stays usable.
• Carry a safety whistle to alert others in case of danger.
• Save emergency contacts on speed dial for quick access.
• Keep some cash, ID proof, and small medical essentials handy.
• A small flashlight or keychain light can be very useful at night.
• Install safety apps like HERHEAVEN that can send your live location instantly.
• Make sure your bag is light enough to carry comfortably if you need to move fast.
